03

Action items & recommendations

1
Fix Footwear economics before chasing more Footwear revenue

Renegotiate cost on low-margin SKUs (Canvas Sneakers at 16%), and treat its returns as a margin emergency, not a service metric. Growing this category as-is grows work, not profit.

$40,168 revenue → $330 kept · $6,924 refunds
2
Attack “wrong size / poor fit” in the Mobile App

Add size guides, a fit finder, and true-to-size measurements to mobile PDPs, concentrated on Footwear. One reason + one channel = the highest-leverage return fix you have.

44% of returns · 59% on Mobile · $5,471
3
Rebalance acquisition toward Referral

Fund and formalize the referral program; audit Paid Social on lifetime value, not signups, before scaling its spend. Pull your best customers from where your best customers already come from.

Referral VIP index 284 · Paid Social 16
4
Close the data gaps that hide the P&L

10 returned orders have no refund record and 18 orders have no product match — so true losses here are slightly understated. Tightening these makes the contribution numbers exact.

10 returns unrecorded · 18 orders unmapped
Method. Orders (927, cleaned) joined to products on ProductName, customers on CustomerID, returns on OrderID. True net contribution = Revenue − (UnitCost × Qty), less refunds; restocked returns credit COGS back; non-restocked returns bear the full cost of goods. Reverse-logistics cost is not included, so returns are conservatively understated.
Coverage & caveats. Cost data present for 909 / 927 orders (18 blank product names excluded from contribution). 99 of 107 returned orders have a refund record; the 8 without are treated as un-refunded, understating loss. VIP index compares each channel's VIP share to its share of the ordering customer base; base n for VIP is 21 customers — directionally strong, worth confirming as the cohort grows.
